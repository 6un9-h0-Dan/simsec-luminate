Insight

When funders wear multiple hats — as individual advocates as well as representatives of a funding organization — it can be unclear which role the funder is playing at any given time.

The power dynamic of the funder-grantee relationship is always present, even when the individuals from organizations are interacting in other contexts. If funders are also going to be active in the spaces where their grantees work, then they need to clearly communicate which role

"You can’t participate in civil society if you are a funder. You never get more than 50% of what is going on."

The confusion is exacerbated by the fact that Luminate sees itself as an “operational funder,” a funding organization that engages in direct advocacy as well as also supporting the policy and advocacy goals of organizations in the field, especially when funders are not considered to practice advocacy on the same level as those organization.
